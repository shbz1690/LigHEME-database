Heme Protein Database

LigHEME is a free database of Heme Proteins. It contains their properties , their pdb ids , uniprot ids , panther ids , resolution , ligand ids and their Scop class / Scop Fold information

What is LigHEME

The LigHEM database provides the scientific community with a high-quality manually curated resource of protein ligand and functional information. The database contains high-quality easily usable functional sites, structure-guided alignments, ligand conformations, evolutionary information on protein families, SNP and disease information. The search engine implemented in the back-end gives this database its power and allows for scientists to do searches and retrieve information that is not easily available from other resources.With the success in genomic initiatives we have also come to appreciate the complexity in defining protein function. It can be defined as a hierarchy at various levels starting at the level of fold down to the level of functional residues. This hierarchical functional classification becomes essential when annotating families of sequences down to a single protein record as is the mission of the Uniprot Consortium. Understanding protein function at various levels becomes very essential when propagating functional information to families with diverse taxonomic groups that carry varied evolutionary histories and minimize the propagation of errors. Hence there is a need for careful and systematic approaches for functional inference. This will also help in the development of prediction algorithms and methodologies and aid in improving the existing methods.

The LigHEME database uses an approach called the Ligand-centric conceived by me some years ago. This ligand-centric approach is multi-pronged as it encompasses analysis at various sequence and structural levels . It is divided into four levels: residue, protein/domain, ligand and family levels. At the residue level it includes identification of conserved binding site residues based on structure-guided sequence alignments of representative members of a PIRSF family and identification of conserved motifs. At the protein/domain level it includes SCOP folds, domain architectures, protein topology, structural elements comprising the binding sites and electrostatics. At the ligand level it includes ligand architectures, ligand conformations, ligand-clustering analysis, ribose sugar puckering and identification of conserved ligand atom interactions. Finally, at the family level it includes phylogenetic analysis.
